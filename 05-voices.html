Section 05

Voices from Within GSG

Designed with the GSG marketing team to answer the questions that matter most before any creative direction is set. Three voices — educator, CEO, operational builder — confirmed the same strategic territory, independently and without prompting.

The research programme

Target: 20–30 staff with 5+ years' tenure. Received: 13 completed surveys across 9 countries and 5 school brands.

Probed: brand perception, values alignment, what makes GSG different in practice vs. on paper.

Awareness gap

Only 38% identified "Learn Limitless"

…as GSG's brand manifesto, unprompted. Strongest among HQ and comms staff; lowest at campus level. An internal activation gap ahead of the September launch.

Consistency

61% see brand presentation vary by campus

Inconsistent visual identity in classrooms and parent-facing materials. Staff recognise the aspiration but cite practical resource gaps at campus level.

Preferred narrative

"Accessible" beat "elite" by 2:1

"High-quality education made accessible" outscored "elite global education" 2:1 — staff across geographies framed GSG's value as excellence without exclusivity.

Where interviews and survey converge — and diverge

Convergences

  • Accessible Excellence confirmed. All three interviewees and survey respondents independently framed GSG's value as excellence without exclusivity.
  • Pride in academic outcomes. Strong, consistent belief that GSG delivers genuine academic quality.
  • Community as advantage. The multicultural, globally connected community is a differentiator staff are proud to articulate.

Divergences

  • Manifesto awareness. Leadership is fluent in "Learn Limitless"; the survey shows campus-level gaps.
  • Consistency vs. lived experience. Interviewees described an aspirationally consistent brand; survey respondents highlighted real inconsistency on the ground.
  • Confidence in technology. Leadership cited SmartLearn confidently; staff show mixed awareness of its deployment and impact.

Strategic reading

The survey confirms Accessible Excellence as the right territory. The divergences are equally instructive: the external claim must be earned internally first. The critical activation risk is not whether the positioning resonates with audiences — it clearly does — but whether staff can carry it with conviction on launch day. Internal alignment shouldn't be a follow-on workstream; it should be concurrent and prerequisite.

Three voices, one direction

The most significant finding of the programme: three independent insiders each confirmed the same strategic territory in their own language — without prompting.

Melissa Maria

Academic Director, GSG

"I've had children who came in labelled as good for nothing from other schools… these are the ones who have graduated and are doing exceedingly well."

Her own formulation: "No child left unattended."

Rajeev Katyal

CEO, Global Schools Foundation

"GSG gives education of world-class standards at affordable prices, and produces students who are designed to become successful in a global world."

His spontaneous one-sentence description of GSG.

Kaustubh Bodhankar

Deputy CEO, Global Schools Foundation

"We are perhaps the only school able to do personalised learning at scale — to pinpoint, through SmartLearn, the learning of each and every individual."

His closing, spontaneous description of GSG.

Where all three converge — without prompting

Accessible Excellence: outstanding, measurable, award-winning academic outcomes — available to any child, at any ability level, without premium pricing or selective entry. The territory is not a competitive hypothesis. It is what all three insiders independently describe as the organisation they have worked in for 15–25 years.

Instinctive language — direct from the interviews

"No child left unattended" "Say what you do, do what you say" "World class at affordable prices" "Personalised learning at scale" "One common people, one common purpose" "Make the dreams of others come true" "Freedom to grow"
