Section 01

The Founder's Vision

Atul Temurnikar's philosophy, synthesised from the kick-off session and earlier conversations. Not marketing positions, but the operating beliefs behind every decision GSG has made — and where the campaign begins.

It started with a real problem, not a grand vision

"There were expatriates of different types, different demographics, different income segments — and the ones who could not afford the pricey international schools were the ones who were feeling the heat."

— Atul Temurnikar, Sun Media interview, May 2025

01

Indian expat families in Singapore needed affordable, curriculum-continuous schooling. No one was providing it.

02

The solution was practical, not aspirational. The ambition came from the demand that followed the quality.

03

Growth was word-of-mouth, pre-social-media. Japan, Malaysia, Thailand — communities found them, not the reverse.

January 2025: the President of India conferred the Pravasi Bharatiya Samman Award — India's highest honour for overseas citizens — on Atul Temurnikar, specifically for his contributions to education in Southeast Asia. Government-level independent validation of the founding mission.

Five core convictions

01

Non-selective is harder — and more impressive

Taking any child and producing outstanding outcomes is a fundamentally harder achievement than filtering for high performers. Selective schools' growth is incremental. Ours is transformational.

02

Technology should protect the child, not replace the human

SmartLearn was built to make the school people-independent — so when a teacher changes, the child's journey is uninterrupted.

Atul: "…the future of edtech is going to be really what happens in the classroom."

03

Community is not a programme. It is who we are.

Singapore National Flag at every campus. Seven-religion prayer. $1.25M+ to SINDA. COVID oxygen concentrators to India. Atul: "We don't brag about it." That authenticity is the proof.

04

Expensive is not quality. Access is.

A waitlist is a signature of short space, not high quality. "Anybody can walk in." The best schools can come from any price segment.

Atul: "It's not a difficult position for any ultra-rich to find a school… but the mid part of society — can they afford to go to ultra-rich schools? That is not the case."

05

Scale without soul is just a weakly linked chain

The aspiration: to be the Ivy League of the school sector — in quality, outcomes and community. Selective growth going forward. Every campus must deliver the highest possible value.

A world first

The Deming Prize

GIIS Tokyo is the first educational institution in the world ever to win the Deming Prize (JUSE, Nov 2025). In 75 years, fewer than ~40 institutions across all industries have won it. The strongest third-party proof GSG holds.

The non-selective result

GIIS Japan ranked No.1 globally by CBSE for highest cohort average — a non-selective school, a six-student cohort. The significance isn't sample size; it's that every student in the cohort achieved the highest level globally.

The proprietary platform

SmartLearn: 23 years of development, a 200+ person tech team, 50 integrated products, 45,000 students deployed.

The 9GEMS holistic framework won the Golden Peacock Award for Innovative Product/Service (Institute of Directors) — the only third-party pedagogy recognition in the portfolio.
