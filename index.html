What this deck establishes

Three findings — and what each means for the brand at its 25th anniversary.

01 · The opportunity is now

15,075 international schools globally (the sector has passed the 15,000 milestone). 58% in Asia. Fee income $69.3B. The local learner is the growth story, and GSG sits at the centre of every trend that matters.

What this means for GSG: the growth is in local families seeking accessible quality — exactly GSG's model.

02 · Competitors avoid the hard claim

Nord Anglia and Inspired dominate premium performance. GEMS owns scale, access and legacy. Cognita and ISP lean holistic. None combine accessible fee positioning with GSG's level of non-selective academic proof.

What this means for GSG: the accessible-excellence territory is unclaimed, and GSG can move to own it.

03 · GSG already occupies it

A 37.2 IBDP average (non-selective). 600+ awards. 25 years. A published manifesto, "The Limitless Experience," and a homepage reading "Learn Limitless" — clearly articulated, but not consistently deployed.

What this means for GSG: own it with proof, not aspiration — 25 years of non-selective results competitors cannot replicate.
